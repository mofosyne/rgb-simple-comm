Dark-Clocked machine-readable

DARK acts as an explicit separator between each colour pulse. Stateless decoder — each colour means the same thing regardless of what came before. Aimed at smartphone cameras and RGB sensors.

Human-Readable for human observers

Designed for a field technician who watches the LED and reads the colour sequence verbally. Cyan excluded (too easily called "light blue"). Green and Red serve as both idle status indicators and byte markers.

Blue 00 Magenta 01 Yellow 10 White 11 Green mark↓0 Red mark↓1
Generator Camera Decoder

Transition-Clocked original approach

The original hand-written design (2016). Data encoded as transitions between colours — the colour change itself is the clock event. No DARK separators; the LED stays lit continuously. Stateful decoder using ring-offset arithmetic.
